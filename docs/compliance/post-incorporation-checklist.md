## Critical — complete in the next 30 days

### 1. Public Officer appointment at SARS — **deadline 2026-05-14**

**Statutory reference:** Tax Administration Act s246.
**Deadline:** one month from incorporation → **2026-05-14** (three weeks from today).
**Default:** the sole director.
**How:** lodged via SARS eFiling under "Registration, Amendments and Verification → Amendments → Company Public Officer". Requires copy of ID and proof of address.
**Why it matters:** without a registered Public Officer, SARS notices have nowhere to go. Auto-assessments, penalties, and compliance notices accumulate silently. Every Tax Clearance Certificate request stalls.
**Status:** ☐ Not started.

### 2. Beneficial Ownership filing at CIPC — **overdue since ~2026-04-28**

**Statutory reference:** Companies Amendment Acts of 2024.
**Deadline:** 10 business days after incorporation → ~2026-04-28 (already passed).
**What to file:** the natural person(s) holding ≥5% effective interest, traced through the shareholder chain. 100% of Social Bounty is held by Sanchez Schreiber Investments; the beneficial owner filing must name the natural person(s) who ultimately control that entity.
**How:** CIPC's Beneficial Ownership portal (`https://eservices.cipc.co.za/`), attach supporting docs (IDs, structure chart).
**Why it matters:** late filings attract penalties; non-filing blocks annual returns and puts the company on the deregistration watchlist.
**Status:** ☐ Not started (likely overdue).

### 3. Annual Returns at CIPC — **reminder for 2027-04-14**

**Statutory reference:** Companies Act s33.
**Deadline:** within 30 business days of the anniversary of incorporation → 2027-04-14 onwards for the first one.
**How:** CIPC eServices; fee scales with turnover. Include AFS (Annual Financial Statements) via iXBRL for 2026/27 onward.
**Why it matters:** two consecutive missed annual returns trigger deregistration proceedings.
**Status:** ☐ Future (not yet due).

## Critical before production launch

### 4. Information Officer registration with the Information Regulator

**Statutory reference:** POPIA s55.
**Default:** the head of the private body (sole director) until delegated in writing.
**How:** free, ~30 minutes via the IR portal (`https://inforegulator.org.za`). Register the Information Officer's details.
**Why it matters:** the Privacy Policy and PAIA Manual both reference an Information Officer. POPIA requires the role to be registered with the IR. Unregistered = non-compliant regardless of internal delegation.
**Status:** ☐ Not started.

### 5. PAIA Manual publication

**Statutory reference:** PAIA s51.
**What to publish:** the manual that forms part of the legal wiki at `/legal/paia-manual`. Must be filed with the IR and made available on the website (both covered by the wiki if the IR filing is done).
**How:** email the PDF to the IR's PAIA office at `PAIAManual@inforegulator.org.za` (confirm current email at filing time) after attorney review.
**Why it matters:** every private body in SA is required to have one. Failure to publish is an offence under PAIA.
**Status:** ☐ Draft exists in legal wiki; not yet filed with IR.

## Recommended before enterprise sales

### 6. B-BBEE EME affidavit

**Who qualifies:** EME = Exempted Micro Enterprise, turnover < R10m/year. Social Bounty is well below this threshold at incorporation.
**Benefit:** automatic Level 4 B-BBEE contributor, unlocks procurement scorecard points with most SA enterprises.
**How:** affidavit signed before a commissioner of oaths (any police station or bank branch). Free. Template at the DTIC website. Valid for 12 months.
**Why it matters:** the moment a mid-market SA customer's procurement process includes B-BBEE scoring (which is increasingly common), an up-to-date EME affidavit is the cheapest possible compliance win.
**Status:** ☐ Not started. Low priority until first enterprise customer surfaces.

**What changes when registered:** platform fees become VAT-inclusive (brands get an input tax credit); Terms + pricing page need updating.
**Status:** ☐ Monitor via quarterly revenue review.
